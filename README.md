# Sauce & Spoon Tablet Rollout

A tabletop tablet menu pilot for a restaurant chain — from sourcing and staff training through
launch, customer feedback, and measurable impact on sales and satisfaction.

## Business Problem

Sauce & Spoon, a small restaurant chain, needed to hit annual growth and expansion targets.
As one lever, they piloted tabletop menu tablets at two locations (Downtown and North) to
test whether self-service ordering could improve guest throughput, reduce wait times, and
grow sales — without hurting the in-restaurant experience.

See [`business-problem.pdf`](./business-problem.pdf) for the full project scenario.

## Approach

1. **Sourcing & Installation** — Sourced tablets and coordinated physical installation
   (including electrician work) at both pilot locations
2. **Content & Systems Integration** — Loaded menus, coupons, and branding onto the tablets,
   and integrated them with the existing POS system
3. **Staff Training** — Built and delivered a training plan for both front-of-house and
   back-of-house staff, managing waitstaff concerns about the new system
4. **Pilot Launch & Feedback** — Went live October 15, then collected customer feedback via
   an end-of-meal survey on the tablets themselves
5. **Iterative Improvement** — Used customer feedback to drive continued staff training,
   regular tablet audits for faulty devices, and food waste investigation
6. **Impact Reporting** — Measured results against baseline and communicated outcomes to
   stakeholders via a closeout report and impact presentation

## Results

- **Sales grew over 20%** since the tablet rollout
- **Average daily guest count increased 10%**
- **Wait times cut by 30 minutes**, with checkout time reduced to under 1 minute
- **Food waste reduced by 50%**, driven by better order accuracy and tracking
- **Customer satisfaction rose from 72% (initial pilot) to 86%** after iterative improvements
